I build computational systems capable of extracting, processing and understanding information. My approaches are grounded in Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. Some of the problems I have worked on include: interpreting and understanding human language in the context of a world model; performing task-specific relation extraction and database population; and automatic summarization.

I live in Boston, MA and can be reached many means including via email, Google Talk <clarkeje@gmail.com>, Skype and LinkedIn.

Previously I was a post-doctoral researcher in Dan Roth's Cognitive Computation Group at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I earned my Ph.D. in Informatics under the supervision of Mirella Lapata at the University of Edinburgh.
