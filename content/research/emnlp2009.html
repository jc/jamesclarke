This paper proposes to acquire representations for machine learning by reading text written to accommodate human learning. We propose a novel form of semantic analysis called _reading to learn_, where the goal is to obtain a high-level semantic abstract of multiple documents in a representation that facilitates learning. We obtain this abstract through a generative model that requires no labeled data, instead leveraging repetition across multiple documents. The semantic abstract is converted into a transformed feature space for learning, resulting in improved generalization on a relational learning task." bibtex: | @inproceedings{Eisenstein:Clarke:Goldwasser:Roth:09, author = {Jacob Eisenstein, James Clarke, Dan Goldwasser and Dan Roth}, title = {Reading to Learn: Constructing Features from Semantic Abstracts}, booktitle = {Proceedings of the Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing (EMNLP-2009)}, pages = {958--967}, year = {2009}, address = {Singapore}, } %}
